What Family Offices Should Know About Commercial Real Estate

Family offices that invest in commercial real estate need a strategy that matches their goals.

When it comes to commercial real estate for family offices, there many types of properties and markets to consider. What may fit one family’s investment strategy, may not work for another. By the same token, the type of property or market area that a family may gravitate towards may not always be the best choice at a given time. Different property types and different markets have changing cycles that will influence the opportunities that are out there. Now more than ever, family offices need to be strategic about commercial real estate. With that in mind, here are some insights on how to look at commercial real estate as an investment option.

Evaluate All Types of Property

While a family may prefer to invest in a certain type of property, the choice may be worth revisiting. To illustrate, family offices traditionally favor apartment buildings. But that market is changing with a current oversupply of multifamily apartments across the United States. This could mean a potential downturn for that type of asset. Plus rising construction costs mean new construction and value-add projects may not be as profitable. What’s more, an oversupply leads to higher vacancy levels — which is never good for landlords.

Let’s turn our attention to how a family office might look for new opportunities in light of these cycles. They may find better choices with other types of properties, such as industrial, retail and hotels. For example, continued growth in e-commerce means the demand for industrial space should remain strong. And not all retail is suffering from the fallout of the internet. Small strip malls offering services you can’t get online, such as coffee shops and beauty services, will continue to be necessary.

Evaluate Local Markets

By the same token, family offices should take a strategic look at locations where new opportunities may be emerging. For instance, cities like Boise, Nashville, and Charlotte, North Carolina may be the new frontier. Conversely, today’s investor may have missed opportunities in Seattle but may still find some potential in Denver.

Some families may prefer to invest in a geographic area they know well. They understand the area, they’re more familiar with the local economy and employment picture. Families who invest in real estate may like to know their tenants, and they may even manage the buildings. But, if they are open to investing in new markets, they may broaden their choice of opportunities.

Monitor Interest Rates and Other Economic Trends

It’s also important to closely monitor factors such as interest rates and other economic trends. Similarly, there should always be concern about where we are in the U.S. economic cycle. Certainly, e-commerce, artificial intelligence and co-working may impact the future of real estate investing. The good news is family offices have the ability to take a long term approach to investing — they can wait things out if necessary.

Ultimately, identifying the best commercial real estate opportunities for a family office is often subjective — some families may be more comfortable with one property over another. By answering the question, “What are we trying to achieve?”, family offices can capitalize on the best opportunities at the right time. With a clearly stated strategy, the investments can match the values and goals of the family members.
